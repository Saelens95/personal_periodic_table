# TITLE: IN ALL THEORITICAL SCIENCES OF REASON, SYNTHETICAL JUDGMENTS A PRIORI ARE CONTAINED AS PRINCIPLES

-> Kant argues that all theoritical sciences rely on synthetic a priori judgments as the fundemental principles.
    -> They expand knowledge without direct experience.

        -> Mathematics - prime example of synthetic a priori knowledge
            -> Ex. 7 + 5 = 12 is not just analytic (contained in definitions of 7 and 5)
                -> They actually extend our knowledge through pure intuition of numbers.

        -> Pure Natural Science (Physics) - its fundemental laws are not purely derived from experience
            -> Though these laws happen to be necessary prinicples that our mind applies to experience.

        -> Metaphysics - attempts to use synthetic a priori knowledge, but Kant questions whether it does so legitimately

    * This section sets up Kant's goal: to establish a rigorous science of pure reason
        -> One that determines how synthetic a priori judgments are possible, especially in fields like mathematics and physics.
    
    * Basically: create a way to explain how we can learn mopre about something without having to experience it first.
