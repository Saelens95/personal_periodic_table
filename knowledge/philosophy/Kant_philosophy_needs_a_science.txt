# NOTES ON THE CRITIQUE OF PURE REASON BY IMMANUEL KANT

# START NOTE 

# TITLE: PHILOSOPHY STANDS IN NEED OF A SCIENCE WHICH SHALL DETERMINE THE POSSIBILITY, PRINCIPLES, AND EXTENT OF HUMAN KNOWLEDGE A PRIORI

-> Kant argues that philosophy requires a systematic science of pure reason to establish limits and foundations of a priori knowledge.
    -> While people use a priori knowledge, philosophy must critically examine it to determine its validity.
        -> Key Question: What can we truly know before experience?
    
    -> Traditional Metaphysics (questions about God, the soul, free will, etc.) relies heavily on a priori reasoning.
        -> Kant believes it often goes beyond what human reason is actually capable of knowing.
    
    
    -> Thus, Kant proposes a "Critique of Pure Reason" -- a rigorous investigation into what reason can and cannot achieve on its own.


# END NOTE
