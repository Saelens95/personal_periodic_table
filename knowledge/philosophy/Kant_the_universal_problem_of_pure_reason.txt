# START NOTE 

# TITLE: THE UNIVERSAL PROBLEM OF PURE REASON

-> Kant frames the central question of his Critique of Pure Reason:
    
-> Can reason purely through thinking (without experiences), attain knowledge that extends beyond what we observe?

-> Metaphysics has always sought to answer questions about God, the soul, freedom (free will), and the universe as a whole
    -> Though, it often leads to contradictions

-> Unlike mathematics and physics, which have clear foundations:
    -> Metaphysics lacks certainty -- leading to endless debates.

* The Goal: to critically examine pure reason itself:
    -> What are the limits of reason?
    -> What can we know independently of experience?
    -> How do synthetic a priori judgments shape our understanding of the world?

* the investigation will determine whether metaphysics can be a legitimate science, or if it is doomed to speculation.
