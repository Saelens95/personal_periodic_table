# NOTES ON THE CRITIQUE OF PURE REASON BY IMMANUEL KANT

# START NOTE 

# TITLE: OF THE DIFFERENCE BETWEEN EMPIRICAL AND PURE KNOWLEDGE:

-> Kant explains that knowledge comes from experience (empirical knowledge) but also from reason alone (pure knowledge).

-> Emipirical Knowledge: comes from sensory experience
    -> it is posteriori -- learned through observation (ex. fire is hot comes from experiencing heat)

-> Pure Knowledge: indepedent of experience
    -> it is a priori -- exists before any sensory input (ex. math -- 2 + 2 = 4, regardless of experience)

-> Kant argues that while empirical knowledge is important, pure knowledge is necessary for understanding the fundemental structure of reality.

-> Certain concepts (e.g, space, time, causality, etc.) must exist in the mind before experience can even make sense. 

-> Kant's big argument is that our minds structure experience using a priori concepts.
    -> We don't just observe reality, we impose catagories on reality in order to make sense of it

# END NOTE
