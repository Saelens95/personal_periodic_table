# NOTES ON THE CRITIQUE OF PURE REASON BY IMMANUEL KANT

# START NOTE 

# TITLE: THE HUMAN INTELLECT, EVEN IN AN UNPHILOSOPHICAL STATE, IS IN POSSESSION OF CERTAIN COGNITIONS A PRIORI:

-> Kant argues that all humans, even without formal philosophical training, naturally possess some a priori knowledge--knowledge independent of experience.
    -> We don't have to learn space and time through experience; 
        -> We already perceive the world within these frameworks;
    
    -> Even before studying philosophy, people instictively assume cause and effect;
        -> We naturally expect that an event has a cause;
    
    -> This means that a priori knowledge is not something we learn, but something our minds are akready structured to use.

-> The human mind actively shapes reality, rather than passively receiving information.
    -> Experience fills in the details, but the framework is already there.

    # END NOTE
